The Impact of Food Wastage

Introduction

Food wastage is a significant global issue with far-reaching impacts on the environment, economy, and society. Each year, approximately one-third of all food produced for human consumption is wasted, amounting to about 1.3 billion tons.

Environmental Impact

Greenhouse Gas Emissions: Wasted food decomposes in landfills, producing methane, a potent greenhouse gas that contributes to climate change.
Resource Waste: Food production consumes vast amounts of water, land, and energy. When food is wasted, these resources are squandered.
Biodiversity Loss: Agriculture expansion to produce more food leads to deforestation and loss of habitats, endangering wildlife.
